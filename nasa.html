Experience Overview

From June 2024 to June 2025, I completed a year-long internship at NASA Ames Research Center through the CHSLE High School and OSTEM College Internship program.

During the internship, I worked on both software and aerospace-related projects while learning how professional teams organize, develop, and present technical work.

State-of-the-Art Satellite Technology Web App

I worked on a five-person team developing a web application based on NASA's State-of-the-Art Small Spacecraft Technology Report.

The goal of the application was to make SmallSat design information easier to explore by allowing users to review NASA data, compare technologies, plan a satellite configuration, and identify parts before building a physical spacecraft.

My main contribution was working on the application's backend logic using JavaScript, while also contributing to the HTML and CSS used throughout the project.

Project Poster

Our team presented the project through a technical poster describing the application's frontend, backend, semantic search tool, graph analysis, feedback system, and 3D visualization features.

ACS3 Solar Sail Model

Alongside the software project, I helped construct a model of NASA's Advanced Composite Solar Sail System, also known as ACS3.

This gave me experience working on a physical aerospace project and helped me better understand how large-scale structures, teamwork, and careful assembly come together in an engineering environment.

What I Learned

I improved my coding skills during the internship, but the biggest lesson was learning how to work as part of a technical team.

Using GitHub, coordinating with four teammates, reviewing changes, and communicating clearly showed me how important collaboration is when building real engineering and software projects.
