Project Overview

I built this drone to get my foot in the door with UAV systems and gain hands-on experience assembling, testing, and flying a quadcopter. My goal was to better understand how the different components of a drone work together as one complete aircraft.

The drone was assembled from a stock DIY kit and took about two hours to complete. The build process was mostly straightforward, although I went back and adjusted the frame after noticing what appeared to be an issue with the weight distribution.

Objective

Gain practical experience building and operating a quadcopter while developing a stronger understanding of UAV systems, electronics, aerodynamics, and flight testing.

Flight Testing

A total of four outdoor flight tests were completed.

  • Flights 1 & 2: Conducted in average wind speeds of 2–5 mph with gusts between 8–9 mph. The aircraft maintained stable flight and responded well to pilot inputs.
  • Flights 3 & 4: Conducted in average winds of 8–9 mph with gusts reaching 10–15 mph. The drone drifted with the wind and struggled to maintain position, demonstrating the effects of stronger winds on lightweight aircraft.
  • Highest estimated altitude reached: approximately 20–25 feet.

During the learning process I experienced several crashes. These were caused by pilot error while developing my flying skills rather than mechanical failures.

Engineering Lessons

This project taught me that weight symmetry is important for stable flight. Even small balance issues can noticeably affect aircraft performance.

I also learned how much environmental conditions influence lightweight UAVs. The drone flew very well in calm weather but became much more difficult to control as wind speeds increased.
