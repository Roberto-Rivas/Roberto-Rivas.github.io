Project Overview

I built this drone to get my foot in the door with UAV systems and gain hands-on experience with how drones are assembled, balanced, tested, and flown.

The aircraft was built from a stock DIY kit and took about two hours to assemble. The build process was mostly straightforward, although I did have to go back and adjust the frame because the weight distribution did not appear balanced.

Objective

Gain practical experience building and operating a quadcopter while developing a better understanding of UAV systems, electronics, aerodynamics, and flight testing.

Flight Testing

A total of four outdoor flight tests were completed.

  • Flight 1 & 2: Average wind speeds of 2–5 mph with gusts reaching 8–9 mph. The drone maintained stable flight and responded well to pilot inputs.
  • Flight 3 & 4: Average wind speeds of 8–9 mph with gusts between 10–15 mph. The aircraft struggled to maintain its position and drifted with the wind, demonstrating how strongly environmental conditions affect lightweight UAVs.
  • Highest estimated altitude reached was approximately 20–25 feet.

During the learning process there were several crashes, but these were the result of pilot error while learning to fly rather than mechanical failures.

Engineering Lessons

One of the biggest lessons from this project was how important weight symmetry is to aircraft stability. Small balance issues can noticeably affect flight performance.

I also learned that wind is one of the biggest challenges for lightweight aircraft. While the drone performed very well in calm conditions, moderate winds and stronger gusts made maintaining position significantly more difficult.
